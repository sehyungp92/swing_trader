### Top 5 Root Causes of Low Trading Frequency

#### 1. Entry Time Restrictions Kill 55% of Valid Signals

**Impact: 203 of 368 signals (55.2%) fire during restricted hours and are discarded.**

The hourly data includes extended-hours bars (pre-market 04:00-09:30 ET and after-hours
16:00-20:00 ET). The signal detection (pullback_signal, indicators) computes on ALL bars,
but `_is_entry_restricted()` blocks entries before 09:45 ET and after 15:50 ET. With ~16
extended-hours bars per day vs ~7 RTH bars, roughly 56% of bar time is restricted, and
55.2% of signals fall in those windows.

This is the #1 bottleneck. The strategy "sees" pullback setups during extended hours but
cannot act on them.

#### 2. Trend Confirmation Delay (44% of days are FLAT)

**Impact: 5,080 hourly bars (26.6%) are in trending regime but have FLAT trend direction.**

The `CONFIRM_DAYS_NORMAL=2` requirement means raw bias must persist for 2 consecutive
daily bars before `trend_dir` becomes non-FLAT. 141 days have raw bias but fail confirmation.
Combined with days in RANGE regime, 44.1% of all days produce zero signals.

The fast-confirm paths (score >= 55, Path C) help somewhat but still require 1 full day.

#### 3. Strict Pullback Signal Conditions (92.8% of touches rejected)

**Impact: From 5,101 pullback EMA touches, only 368 (7.2%) generate a full signal.**

`pullback_signal()` requires ALL conditions simultaneously on the same 1H bar:
- `low <= ema_pull` (price touches EMA)
- `close > ema_pull` (price recovers above EMA)
- `close > ema_mom` (momentum confirmation)
- `close > prior_high` (prior-bar confirmation)

The shadow tracker shows `prior_high_confirm` rejects 416 candidates with +0.388R avg
shadow R, meaning this filter blocks profitable setups. The same-bar touch+recovery
requirement is also very restrictive with 1H bar granularity.

#### 4. Position Occupancy Blocks 25% of Remaining Signals

**Impact: 94 of 368 signals (25.5%) fire while a position is already open.**

The strategy only generates candidates when `position.direction == Direction.FLAT`. With
avg hold time of 127 hours (~5.3 trading days), a quarter of post-restriction signals
are wasted because the strategy is already in a trade.

#### 5. Breakout Entry Path Contributes Zero Trades

**Impact: The entire Candidate B (breakout arm-then-pullback) system produces 0 trades
for QQQ over the full 5-year period.**

The breakout entry requires STRONG_TREND regime (ADX >= 30) + Donchian breakout + subsequent
pullback to EMA_mom — a very narrow set of conditions. For QQQ, STRONG_TREND occurs only
20.6% of days, and the arm-then-pullback sequence never completes within the 12-hour arm
window.

### Recommendations

#### Structural Changes (high impact, require code changes)

1. **Filter hourly data to RTH-only bars before signal detection** — This is the single
   highest-impact change. By computing indicators and signals only on RTH bars (09:30-16:00 ET),
   all 368 signals would fire during tradeable hours. Expected impact: roughly 2× more
   tradeable signals (from ~165 to ~368). This requires changes to `backtest/data/preprocessing.py`
   or the engine's bar loop.

2. **Relax the same-bar touch+recovery requirement** — Allow the pullback touch and recovery
   to span 2-3 bars instead of requiring both conditions on the same bar. This would dramatically
   increase the 7.2% touch-to-signal conversion rate. Requires changes to `strategy/signals.py`
   `pullback_signal()`.

3. **Remove or relax `close > prior_high` confirmation** — Shadow data shows +0.388R avg for
   rejected candidates. This filter is net costly. The ablation flag exists but the signals
   it generates are lost to entry time restrictions (cause #1) before they can become trades.
   Fixing cause #1 first would make this ablation meaningful.

#### Parameter Tuning (moderate impact, config-only changes)

4. **Reduce `CONFIRM_DAYS_NORMAL` from 2 to 1** — The optimizer's best trial uses
   `confirm_days_normal=1`. This would confirm trends 1 day faster, making 141 additional
   days available for signals. Change in `strategy/config.py`.

5. **Consider wider stops** — The optimizer consistently prefers wider stops (daily_mult
   2.8 vs 2.1, hourly_mult 4.3 vs 2.7). Wider stops mean fewer premature stop-outs and
   longer holds, but fewer overall entries. This is a quality-vs-frequency trade-off that
   favors quality.

#### Monitoring Changes (low priority)

6. **Fix the optimizer's `min_total_trades=200` for single-symbol runs** — The default
   objective in `backtest/optimization/objective.py` requires 200 trades minimum, making
   all single-symbol optimization trials invalid. Add a single-symbol override or
   scale the threshold by number of symbols.

7. **Investigate breakout entry path** — If the breakout arm-then-pullback system is
   intended to contribute trades, the `ARM_WINDOW_HOURS=12` or the STRONG_TREND
   requirement may be too restrictive. Consider widening the arm window or allowing
   breakouts during TREND regime.

### Key Finding: Trade Count Unchanged

**All four changes are implemented correctly and working**, but trade count remains at
29 because the **#1 bottleneck was not addressed**: the entry time restriction at
`backtest_engine.py:973` (`_is_entry_restricted()`).

The entry time restriction blocks signals before 09:45 ET and after 15:50 ET. In the
original codebase, this blocked 55.2% of signals (203 of 368) that fired during
extended-hours bars. The RTH filter removed extended-hours bars from the data, but the
remaining 09:00-09:44 ET bars are still computed and still rejected by the entry restriction.

| Change | Working? | Expected Impact | Actual Impact | Why |
|--------|----------|-----------------|---------------|-----|
| RTH-only filter | Yes | ~2× signals | ~0% | Removed extended-hours bars, but signals that would have fired at 09:00-09:44 are still rejected by entry restriction. RTH filter changed indicator values slightly (no noise from pre/post-market), yielding minor price differences. |

### Remaining Bottleneck: Entry Time Restriction

The entry time restriction at `backtest_engine.py:970-974` is the binding constraint:

```python
def _generate_candidates(self, h, d, bar_time):
    if self._is_entry_restricted(bar_time):
        return  # ← EARLY RETURN: signals not even computed
```

`_is_entry_restricted()` blocks entries before 09:45 ET and after 15:50 ET. With the
RTH filter producing bars from 09:00-15:59, only bars from 09:45-15:49 can generate
entries — a ~6-hour window. The 09:00-09:44 bars (3-4 bars/day) are computed for
indicators but cannot trigger entries.

### Next Steps to Actually Increase Frequency

1. **Relax entry time restriction** — Allow entries from 09:30 (market open) instead of
   09:45. The 15-minute delay was a risk-management choice but blocks early-session
   pullback setups. Alternatively, remove the restriction entirely since RTH filtering
   already ensures only market-hours bars are processed.

2. **Address the 92.8% touch-to-signal loss** — The pullback signal still requires
   `close > ema_pull` AND `close > ema_mom` on the same bar as (or within 3 bars of)
   the EMA touch. The multi-bar touch helped the touch detection but not the recovery
   conditions. Consider relaxing the `close > ema_mom` requirement or allowing recovery
   across a wider window.

3. **Reduce position occupancy blocking** — 25.5% of signals were blocked by an existing
   open position (avg hold 127 hours). Tighter trailing stops or time-based exits could
   free up the strategy for new entries sooner.
